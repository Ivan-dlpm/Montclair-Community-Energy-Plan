# ⚡ Montclair Community Energy Plan – Community Overview

**Township of Montclair – Department of Sustainability | Data Analytics Internship**
**January – May 2026**

---

## Project Overview

This project contributes a data-driven Community Overview to the Township of Montclair's Community Energy Plan — a municipal planning document developed under a New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU) Community Energy Plan Grant. The overview synthesizes community-scale greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions data, electric vehicle adoption trends, rooftop solar potential, housing characteristics, and demographic equity gaps to establish the baseline conditions for Montclair's clean energy strategy.

## Topics Covered

### 🏘️ Community Profile & Demographics
- Population: 38,634 (2020) → 39,873 (2023)
- Racial/ethnic diversity breakdown
- Median household income trends ($134,308 → $166,765, 2020–2023)
- Identification of overburdened communities under NJDEP criteria

### 🏠 Housing Characteristics & Energy Implications
- 60% owner-occupied / 40% renter-occupied split
- Over 9,000 housing units built in 1939 or earlier — significant retrofit potential
- Weatherization, electrification, and Home Performance with ENERGY STAR opportunities
- Equity barriers for renters and LMI (low- and moderate-income) households

### 🚗 Transportation Patterns & Emissions
- Transportation identified as the **largest contributor** to Montclair's GHG emissions
- 44% of residents commute by driving alone; 32% work from home; 16% use public transit
- Rapid EV adoption trajectory supported by state incentives and local infrastructure investment
- Vision Zero Task Force, Bike Bus program, and senior mobility initiatives

### ☀️ Renewable Energy & Solar Potential
- Google Project Sunroof analysis: 113 MW of rooftop solar capacity across 8,300 viable rooftops
- Median viable rooftop: 317 sq ft, 7.6 kW DC capacity, ~8,200 kWh/year
- Community solar development as key pathway for renters and LMI residents
- Township-authorized RFPs for municipal-hosted community solar projects

### 🏛️ Municipal Operations
- Participation in the Local Government Energy Audit (LGEA) program since 2016
- Municipal fleet electrification initiatives underway
- Capital planning aligned with NJ Clean Energy Program incentives

## Tools & Methods

- **Data Sources:** U.S. Census Bureau (ACS), Sustainable Jersey, Google Project Sunroof, NJBPU, NJ DCA Municipal Revitalization Index
- **Analysis:** GHG emissions trend analysis, EV adoption tracking, solar feasibility assessment, demographic and housing equity analysis
- **Tools Used:** Excel (data compilation and modeling), ArcGIS Pro (spatial context), Google Project Sunroof Data Explorer
- **Output Format:** Policy-ready community overview document delivered to township sustainability staff

---

## Equity Focus

A core objective of this analysis was identifying **equity gaps in energy program access**:

- Renter households (40% of housing stock) face structural barriers to rooftop solar due to split-incentive dynamics between landlords and tenants
- 1,112 households have no vehicle access, underscoring the importance of robust public transit alongside EV policy
- Community solar prioritized as the primary mechanism for LMI household participation
- Certain Montclair neighborhoods identified as overburdened communities under NJDEP criteria
